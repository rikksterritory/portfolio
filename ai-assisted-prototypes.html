Context

Why I started building before wireframing

Instead of waiting until high-fidelity design, I use vibe-coded prototypes to make the idea tangible early. This helps stakeholders, engineers, and champion users react to the actual experience before I spend more time designing or building the wrong thing.

Design judgment

Where my role matters most

AI can help generate interfaces, code, and variations quickly. But the value still comes from product judgment: choosing the right problem, deciding what to test, identifying what users need to trust, and knowing when a prototype is strong enough to move forward.

This page reflects how I use AI as a design partner - not to skip thinking, but to make thinking visible earlier.

Problem framing

Choosing the right user problem before generating anything.

Interaction judgment

Deciding what needs to be clicked, tested, explained, or controlled.

AI behavior design

Defining what AI should do, suggest, summarize, or hand off.

Stakeholder alignment

Using prototypes to make decisions easier across product, design, and engineering.

Early validation

This is how I reduce ambiguity before design gets expensive.

For me, vibe coding is not about replacing design craft. It is a way to move from discussion to interaction faster - so teams can test ideas, challenge assumptions, and make better product decisions earlier.
